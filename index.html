Welcome to Eko Cuisines

Eko (Lagos) cuisine is diverse and flavorful. Key dishes include Amala and Ewedu, Ofada Rice, and Ewa Agoyin. Popular street foods are Suya, Akara, and Bole. You'll find everything from local "bukka" eateries to upscale restaurants offering various Nigerian and international foods. Explore local neighborhoods and street stalls for an authentic taste!

Discover the rich and diverse flavors of Eko Cuisines, where every dish tells a story.

Our Cuisines

    Amala and Ewedu Soup

    Amala and Ewedu Soup: This is arguably one of the most iconic Eko dishes. Amala is a swallow food made from dried yam flour, giving it a dark brown color. It's typically eaten with Ewedu soup (made from jute leaves) and a spicy stew (obe).

  • Amala And Ewedu soup

    • Ofada Rice

    Ofada Rice: Also known as local rice, Ofada rice is a popular choice in Eko. It's often less processed than regular white rice and has a distinct aroma. It's commonly served with Ofada sauce, a flavorful pepper sauce made with palm oil, locust beans (iru), and various meats.

  • Ofada Rice

    • Ewa Agoyin

    Ewa Agoyin: This is a popular street food made from mashed beans cooked until very soft. It's usually served with a spicy pepper sauce made with palm oil, dried pepper, and sometimes fish. It's often paired with Agege bread, a popular type of bread in Eko.

  • Ewa Agoyin

    • Akara (Beans Cakes) and Eko

    Akara (Beans Cakes) and Eko: Akara is made from deep-fried bean paste, and Eko is a smooth. They are often eaten together for breakfast or as a snack.

  • Akara

    • Suya

    This is a must-try Eko street food. Suya consists of thinly sliced marinated meat (usually beef, ram, or chicken) grilled over an open flame and generously seasoned with a spicy peanut-based spice mix.

  • Suya

    • Boli

    Boli: Grilled plantain is a common snack or side dish in Eko. It's often roasted over charcoal until the skin is charred and the inside is soft and sweet. It can be eaten with roasted fish (Boli and Fish) or Ground Nuts.

  • Boli
